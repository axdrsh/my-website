title: 'Why Fedora is my all time favourite linux distro?'
publishedAt: '2024-08-08'

As a long-time Linux user, I've tried out countless different Linux distributions. From Mint to Arch, But through it all, there has always been one distro that has consistently been my favorite - Fedora Linux.

There are so many reasons why I love Fedora, but the first and foremost is the incredible stability and reliability of the operating system. Fedora is based on the cutting-edge development version of the Linux kernel and core system libraries, but Red Hat's team of developers does an amazing job of thoroughly testing and refining everything before each new release. As a result, Fedora always feels rock-solid and dependable, with very few bugs or issues to speak of.

At the same time, Fedora is also remarkably up-to-date. Rather than sticking with older, more stale packages, the distro is constantly integrating the latest versions of all the core system components and applications. This means you get to take advantage of the newest features and performance improvements without having to sacrifice stability. It's the best of both worlds.

Of course, no Linux distro is perfect, and Fedora is no exception. One area where it could be improved is in the realm of multimedia support. Out of the box, Fedora does not include any codecs for playing back common audio and video formats like MP3 and H.264. This can be a bit annoying, but it's easily fixable by enabling the RPM Fusion repositories.

Another potential downside of Fedora is that it has a relatively short support lifecycle compared to some other distros. New versions are released on a 6-month cadence, and each is only officially supported for about 13 months. This means you have to upgrade to a new version relatively frequently if you want to keep getting security updates.

But for me, these minor drawbacks are far outweighed by all the things that Fedora gets right. The rock-solid stability, cutting-edge software, commitment to free and open-source ideals, and excellent software curation make it an absolute joy to use day-to-day. That's why Fedora has been and will always be my number one Linux distro of choice. Thanks for reading.
